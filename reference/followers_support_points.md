# Followers and Combat: Support Points Reference

## How Support Points Work

1. For each hireling (NPC follower) traveling with your party, you gain **one support point**  
2. Support points can be spent during combat to give yourself a benefit — narratively, the benefit represents a hireling performing some action in the background  
3. Once a support point is spent, it cannot be used again for a certain amount of time (determined by risk level and a d20 roll)  
4. The potency of the benefit carries a proportional **risk** — higher-risk actions bring higher rewards but also lock out support points for longer periods  
5. High-risk actions may cause hirelings to **die** — if that happens, you lose that support point **permanently**; hirelings are rarely willing to return to life

---

## Risk Level Summary

| Risk Level | Roll | Cooldown on Bad Result | Cooldown on Good Result |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Low** | d20; roll 1 \= bad | Short or long rest cooldown | End of current encounter |
| **Medium** | d20; roll 5 or lower \= bad | Long rest cooldown | Short or long rest |
| **High** | d20; roll 10 or lower \= **DEATH** (permanent loss) | — | Long rest cooldown |

---

## Low-Risk Actions

Low-risk actions provide the smallest benefit but are the most reliable. There is a chance the follower is distracted at a critical moment and knocked out of the fight. On a roll of **1**, the support point doesn't refresh until the party finishes a short rest. Otherwise, it refreshes after the end of the current encounter.

### Offensive

- Add **\+1d4** to your attack roll, OR subtract **1d4** from an enemy saving throw

### Defensive

- When hit by an attack or forced to make a saving throw, gain **\+2 to AC or the saving throw** — potentially turning a hit into a miss or a failed save into a success

### Utility

- Gain **advantage on your next ability check**

---

## Medium-Risk Actions

Medium-risk actions put your hireling in enough danger that they are guaranteed to suffer some injury. On a roll of **5 or lower**, the support point doesn't refresh until the party completes a long rest. Otherwise, it doesn't refresh until a short or long rest.

### Offensive

- Gain **advantage on your next attack roll**, OR apply **disadvantage to one enemy saving throw**

### Defensive

- When an enemy hits you with an attack, **force them to reroll and take the lower result**; OR when you fail a saving throw, **reroll and take the higher result**

### Utility

- Once on your turn, take a single **free five-foot step** that does not provoke attacks of opportunity and does not count against your movement. Cannot be used if your speed is 0 or you are incapacitated.

---

## High-Risk Actions

High-risk actions are nearly suicidal. A follower will certainly suffer a major injury and their efforts may seal their fate. On a roll of **10 or lower**, the hireling **dies** and you lose that support point permanently. On a roll above 10, the support point doesn't refresh until the party completes a long rest.

### Offensive

- Gain **advantage on your next attack roll**, and if you hit, the attack is **automatically a critical hit**; OR apply **disadvantage to an enemy saving throw** — if they fail, re-roll damage dice and take the higher number

### Defensive

- When an enemy hits you with an attack, **turn that hit into a miss**; OR when you fail a saving throw, **turn the failure into a success**

### Utility

- Transfer one item in your possession to any friendly creature (including yourself) within **60 ft.** — if the item is consumable, the hireling can administer it to the target. This transfer works **even if you are incapacitated**.

## Follower Benefits and Risk: Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

### Cooldown Summary

- **Low Risk:** Roll d20 — 1 \= short/long rest cooldown; \>1 \= end of encounter cooldown  
- **Medium Risk:** Roll d20 — 5 or lower \= long rest cooldown; \>5 \= short or long rest cooldown  
- **High Risk:** Roll d20 — 10 or lower \= **DEATH**; \>10 \= long rest cooldown

### Offensive Options

- **(Low Risk):** \+1d4 to attack roll, or −1d4 to enemy saving throw  
- **(Medium Risk):** Advantage on one attack roll, or enemy saving throw at disadvantage  
- **(High Risk):** Advantage on one attack roll; automatically crit if it hits; OR enemy saving throw at disadvantage — if they fail, re-roll damage dice once and take higher result

### Defensive Options

- **(Low Risk):** When hit by an attack or fail a save, \+2 to AC or saving throw  
- **(Medium Risk):** When hit by an attack, force enemy to re-roll and take lower result; OR when you fail a save, re-roll and take higher result  
- **(High Risk):** When hit by an attack, turn that hit into a miss; OR when you fail a saving throw, turn the failure into a success

### Utility Options

- **(Low Risk):** Advantage on an ability check  
- **(Medium Risk):** Free five-foot step on your turn that does not provoke opportunity attacks and does not count against your movement; cannot use if speed is 0 or you are incapacitated  
- **(High Risk):** Transfer one item to any friendly creature within 60 ft.; if consumable, hireling can administer it to the target; works even if you are incapacitated
